How Has the National Basketball Association Changed in the Past 40 Years?

By Nihar Majmudar

National Basketball Association (NBA) players come in all types of skill levels. From the 1st pick in the draft to free agents picked up by teams halfway throughout the season, it is clear that every player provides different value for a team. A big question that has arisen over the past decade is how the style of these players has changed in the NBA of this era compared to the prior eras. Some trends are clear, however some are more hidden. The change in the styles of play from these players have resulted in massive changes to the team aspects of basketball. In this tutorial I hope to show insights on how the game of basketball, has changed in the NBA over the past 40 years.

Cleaning our Data¶

The first table I cleaned was the NBA averages over time table. The first thing I did was remove rows that contained labels. I did this because the labels were the same as the labels at the top of the current table, they were just included multiple times on the site I extracted it from. Next I dropped all stats that aren't really the focus of the tutorial. Stats like the Age, Height, Weight, and MP per game for teams aren't really relevant in seeing how the NBA has changed over time because most of these stay very consistent over time. The biggest change was dropping all years before the 3pt line was created. I did this because the addition of a 3pt line marks a transition into the "modern NBA era" (this era is split up into many era in between) as it has now become one of the biggest parts of the game. Comparing the league with the 3pt line to one without it wouldn't be as fruitful and many of the contentious debates focus on the league with a 3pt line. To read more about how the 3pt line changed the game of basketball, read here.

The next table I didn't have to clean as much because I only collected years from the site after the 3pt line was created.

Before we start...¶

Let's talk about what all the stats we will be using mean. It is important to remember that all the stats we are looking at are per game averages (whether for all NBA teams in the "nba_averages" or specific NBA teams in the "teams" table)

3PA - The amount of 3 pointers a team shoots
3P - The amount of 3 pointers a team makes
FG- The amount of Field Goals made (includes 3 pointers)
FGA - Amount of Field Goals attempted (includes 3 pointers)
AST - Amount of assists(an assist occurs when a player passes to someone on their team and they make a shot because of that pass)
STL - Amount of steals (a steal is when a team takes possession of the ball from the other team). A higher number is better, it indicates better defensive play from a team.
TOV - Amount of turnovers (turnover occurs when a team loses possession of the ball for reasons other than shooting). A lower number is better, as it means teams are being more careful with the ball.
PF - Amount of personal fouls a team commits
PTS - Amount of points scored
FG% - Field Goal Percetnage = FG/FGA (higher percentage is better)
3P% - 3 point percentagev = 3P/3PA (higher percentage is better)
eFG% - Efficent field goal percentage adjusts for the fact that every 3 point attempt is worth more than a 2 point attempt (higher percentage is better)
ORtg - Points produced per 100 possessions (higher number is better)

If interested, you can find more about these statistics here.

Observing these key statistics¶

When looking at these NBA averages over time, it's important to find what they mean in relation to how the game is played and how the game has changed

I decided to look at some key metrics over time such as average 3 point shot attempts per game, FG%, 3 point %, Assists per game, points per game, turnovers per game, and personal fouls per game. Let's see what we can trends we can try and conclude from this preliminary look at our nba averages data.

  • For the 3PA chart we can we that 3 pointers are increasingly becoming a huge factor in NBA games. When the line was first introduced teams were shooting less than 5 3's a game while today team's are averaging over 30 3 point attempts per game.

  • For FG% there is a dip from the older eras. Maybe we can attribute this to the increase in 3 point shot attempts which are much harder to much compared to 2 point shot attempts.

  • 3P% has obviously gotten higher from when it was first introduced and has hovered around the 35% range for the last 20 years.

  • PTS per game started at around 110 and dipped in the late 90's to early 2000's. Maybe this can tell us something about the defense played in these eras (better defense could lead to less points).

  • Turnovers have seemed to decrease almost every year, this can tell us something about the skill level of players nowadays or maybe even how defenses have changed over time.

  • Personal fouls have seemed to decreased over time also. Once again this could tell us something about the defense and physicality of the game. More physical games often lead to more fouls.

  • Free throw attempts have decreased significantly over time, we can maybe attribute this to the increase in 3 point shooting as there is less contact (and therefore less chance of a foul) when shooting a 3 compared to going inside the paint (an area that is closer to the basket)

  • eFG% is what NBA teams have been chasing to increase. Effective Field Goal percentage takes into account that ever 3 point attempt is worth more than a 2. Seeing how 3PA correlates with this could provide insight into why the NBA has evolved to shooting more 3s.

  • ORtg seems to not have changed much over the years, but we can see in the last 5 years there has been an increase in Offensive Rating which might be able to be attributed to the rise in 3PA

Insights between stat correlation¶

FG% vs 3PA - From this plot we can see that generally, as the amount of 3 point attempts has increased, the average field goal percentage has fallen. This will bring up an interesting observation when we compare it to eFG% vs 3PA.

FTA vs PF - This plot shows what we expect, that as Personal Fouls increase, the amount of Free Throws increase. However, something interesting to note is that PF has and FTA have decreased over time, however points don't reflect this drop in free throw attempts. The amount of points is starting to rise again.

TOV vs AST - This plot shows that as assists have increased, the amount of turnovers has also increased. Since both of these statistics have decreased over time, one observation we can make is that the NBA might be heading towards a less team-oriented, and more isolation like game.

eFG% vs 3PA - This plot has a very interesting takeaway. We saw that FG% has decreased as 3PA, but effective field goal percentage actually increased as 3 point attempts have increased. The reason this is so important is that effective field goal % accounts for the fact that every 3 pointer is worth more than a 2 pointer, meaning that those shots are more valuable. It shows "the percentage a 2 pointer shooter would have to shoot to match the output of a player that also shoots 3". The eFG% is rising to above 50% recently which is higher than any season's FG%. This shows that 3 point shooting is actually more efficient for teams in terms of point output.

ORtg vs eFG% - Although there looks like there isn't a strong correlation between ORtg and 3PA, we can see as the number of 3PA dramatically increases to the above 25 range, the ORtg for these years averages are at all time highs

def f(row):
    if row['Season'] >= 1980 and row['Season'] < 1990:
        val = 1980
    elif row['Season'] >= 1990 and row['Season'] < 2000:
        val = 1990
    elif  row['Season'] >= 2000 and row['Season'] < 2010:

    elif row['Season'] >= 2010:
        val = 2010
    return val
